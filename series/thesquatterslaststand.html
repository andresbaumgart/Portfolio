// THE SQUATTERS' LAST STAND

The government of Nepal plans to destroy illegally built housings in Kathmandu.
On November 17th, thousands of inhabitants of these housings as well as local and international human rights organizations demonstrated against the planned bulldozing of the slum. However, the majority of Kathmandu's legitimate citizens and the media quietly criticize the squatters since they feel disadvantaged. The capital's traffic has come to a standstill for hours. Thus, the usual economy has been interrupted to the detriment of people working in the city. This worsened the already poor public opinion on the issue.

Retrospectively, the demonstration itself was deemed ineffective. The government ignored the efforts and set a deadline of 15 days for the squatters to leave the area. After several weeks of negotiating and adjournments, the area has been bulldozed, the inhabitants made homeless.
