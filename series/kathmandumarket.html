// KATHMANDU MARKET

Kathmandu is the site of uncountable small market places spread all over the city. Locals as well as merchants from the Nepali hinterland and even New Delhi sell meat, fish, vegetables, live animals and other goods. Since Nepal is landlocked and has a minimal distance of roughly 500 km to the Indian ocean, some markets, such as the fish market featured in this story, open either early in the morning or late at night due to the difficult import strategy. Live animals, such as goats, are being weight, sold and transported to the outskirts of Kathmandu.

Since supermarkets and other grocery facilities are rare and almost unaccessible, locals generally buy their goods at market places, making them an essential factor in the people's daily lives and economy. The capital also serves as a transshipment point, not only for Nepal, but for the Himalayan region, including Tibet. A trade route exists between New Delhi, Kathmandu and Lhasa. The one between the latter two is called friendship Highway, financed by the Chinese government.
