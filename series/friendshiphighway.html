// FRIENDSHIP HIGHWAY - 中尼公路

In 1967, the Chinese government completed a new route connecting Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal, with Lhasa. The route begins at the border between the Tibet Autonomous Region (TAR) and Nepal and ends in the Tibetan capital. The highway, commonly referred to as Friendship Highway, is the major trade route in the region.
This set of photographs offers twenty-five insights into the Tibetan landscape and people from the Nepali-Chinese border town Zhangmu, along the Friendship Highway through the valley of Shigatse, into the heart of Lhasa.
