// FUEL PRIZE RISE IN NEPAL

Paralleling the international market situation, the value of fuel in Nepal rose enormously in recent years. Long queues in front of gas stations have become a daily occurrence all over the country. On January 20th, 2012, a 15 percent rise in gas and petrol prices has prompted thousands of Nepali citizens to take to the streets once again. Starting off in a local college in the very centre of the city, the protesters - mainly consisting of students - marched through the streets of Kathmandu. Military and police activity has been exceptionally high even by Nepali standards.

The entire demonstration has been accompanied by riot police and went on peaceful until the wrought-up crowd entered the main shopping district near Durbar Square. Shop owners pulled down their shutters in order to protect their businesses from material damage. Aggressive individuals began smashing shop windows of the first floor and upwards. Police reacted with direct arrests. Half an hour later it came to a confrontation of a greater group of riot police and the front of the demonstration, resulting in police forces using violence against demonstrators, of whom some retaliated by throwing rocks and bricks at the authorities. As soon as partially heavily armed riot police tried to track down violence- using individuals, those disappeared in the masses of people of the nearby Ratnapark marketplace. Many locals blame international oil companies for the rise in prices. Western companies such as Texana are said to control the national oil resources and regulate the market to their advantage.
